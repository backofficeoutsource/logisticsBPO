<h2>What Does a Freight Audit Provider Do?</h2>

<p>
A freight audit provider validates your transportation invoices by checking each freight bill for accuracy against contracted rates, tariffs, fuel charges, discounts, and accessorial fees. They also ensure invoice compliance across regions, modes, and carriers (e.g., parcel, LTL, FTL, international air & ocean). After validation, they flag errors, short-pay incorrect charges, and manage the payment process on your behalf.
</p>

<p>
Core capabilities typically include:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Automated invoice capture and processing</li>
  <li>Rate validation and contract compliance</li>
  <li>Duplicate billing and charge dispute management</li>
  <li>Shipment tracking and documentation matching</li>
  <li>Freight payment services and remittance processing</li>
  <li>Billing dispute resolution with carriers</li>
  <li>Analytics and reporting dashboards for transportation spend</li>
</ul>

<p>
For organizations shipping thousands of orders per month, these capabilities significantly reduce errors and processing workload.
</p>

<h2>Why Companies Partner With Freight Audit Providers</h2>

<p>
The primary benefit of using an external freight audit provider is cost control. Shipping invoices contain human and system errors that frequently go unnoticed—fuel surcharges, reclassification fees, weight adjustments, dimensional charges, and late-delivery penalties are common examples. Industry studies show that manual or unmanaged freight billing can contain 3% to 8% in overcharges.
</p>

<p>
Beyond cost savings, companies gain:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Operational efficiency</strong> through reduced manual data entry and invoice reviews</li>
  <li><strong>Faster payment cycles</strong> with verified payment workflows</li>
  <li><strong>Improved carrier relationships</strong> due to dispute handling and clean remittances</li>
  <li><strong>Finance and logistics alignment</strong> via unified transportation data</li>
  <li><strong>Better budgeting</strong> through detailed freight spend visibility</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Evaluate a Freight Audit Provider</h2>

<p>
Choosing the right provider depends on your business size, modes of transport, geographic coverage, and IT capabilities. Below are key evaluation criteria:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Integration capabilities:</strong> Can they integrate with TMS, ERP, WMS, and accounting systems?</li>
  <li><strong>Global coverage:</strong> Do they support multi-currency, cross-border compliance, and regional carriers?</li>
  <li><strong>Analytics & reporting:</strong> Do they offer dashboards to uncover routing inefficiencies and cost trends?</li>
  <li><strong>Technology maturity:</strong> Do they use automation and AI for invoice validation?</li>
  <li><strong>Dispute resolution:</strong> Do they negotiate chargebacks with carriers on your behalf?</li>
  <li><strong>Scalability:</strong> Can they handle peak season volume without delays?</li>
</ul>

In-house processes give control but require staffing, systems, integrations, and audit expertise. Outsourcing shifts the operational burden to specialists who already manage dispute resolution workflows, audit tools, and payment relations with carriers.
